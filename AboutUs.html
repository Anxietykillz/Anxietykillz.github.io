Who is Anxietykillz?

For over 20 years yoga has been an important part of my life. My practice has helped me grow stronger, more fearless and more mindfully aware, on the mat and in my life. 

And then I sneezed. It was 2014 and my ability to physically practice changed drastically in a split second. Chronic debilitating pain and spasms that made me cry took over from perfected headstands (Sirsasana) and Camel Pose (Ustrasana). I went from running as part of my daily meditation strategy to needing walking sticks because of permanent mobility and balance issues.

Bye-bye deep backbends - Hello to a deeper more inclusive practice and relationship with Yoga.

I went looking for experts to help me gain back muscle and strength after surgery. Specialists in Physical Therapy, Osteotherapy, Chiropractic (lifetime patient), and “advanced yoga” teachers looking for relief and compassion. The cost of working out under constant guidance, there to calm my screaming spasms and catch the problem mechanics was out of control. I needed someone who combined these specialties with Yoga theory and psychology.

Most Yoga studios I contacted weren’t comfortable with the risks associated with my new disability.

Deflated...but not for long.

Armed with my research, with the support of my incredible Doctor, I enrolled in pain school offered by our local hospital Chronic Pain Clinic.

But I really didn’t belong there.

Most clients were having a much harder time adapting to their pain and many lived with anxiety and depression as constant companions. While I was always turning to prayer, meditation, and breathwork learned from decades of my Yoga practice, I was still not finding comfort from my pain. Suddenly, during a neuroscience session at the clinic, something inside light up and my brain changed gears.

My brain was forever changed because of pain.

This meant I had to change my relationship with my pain and my brain.

In that moment, I vowed I would forevermore take each breath as it came. 
Yoga reminds me to live each moment more mindfully and more aware:
- of the world
- of the weather
- of my body's needs.

Which means living with a lot less pain in my everyday life.

In my sessions, I love to combine movement sequences that are inclusive of all abilities, mindfulness exercises, breathwork, laughter, and some real talk - all to help deepen your relationship with yourself and Yoga.

Most importantly, I help people living with pain by sharing different tools to put in your Pain Toolkit. Guiding your physical practices, or resiliency and mindset coaching, I can help you to look at your relationship with pain differently too.

Visit Accessibility and Anti-Ableism Coaching to find out more and how I leverage my passion for technology and my experience in diversity and inclusion.

I will always be a student of the deeper practices and humbled for the loving wisdom my teachers and mentors have shared with me.  


Yours in gratitude,

Victoria Patacairk
